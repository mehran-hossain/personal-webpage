Meowzart: Virtual 2D Pet

Overview

Meowzart is a 2D virtual pet application currently in progress, developed as part of my Aging and Technology course.

I’ll be demoing this at a community center for older adults, where I’m exploring how short, interactive experiences can feel meaningful and emotionally engaging.

Motivation

I’ve always loved animals, and I was interested in exploring whether aspects of caregiving and emotional connection from real pet interactions can translate into a simple digital experience.

There are existing examples like Nintendogs or companion robots like Joy for All, but many focus on realism or novelty. With Meowzart, I wanted to take a simpler approach and focus on caregiving, responsiveness, and trust-building.

Approach

One challenge with this idea is that bonds with pets usually develop over time, while the study setting only allows for short interactions (around 10 minutes).

To address this, I designed the experience so users can:

  • cycle through different “days”, showing progression over time
  • interact through both actions and conversation

The chat feature will be implemented using a Wizard-of-Oz approach, allowing me to simulate responsive and emotionally aware interactions during the study without building a full backend system.

In the demo, this appears as gradually building trust with the pet, a simplified way to simulate longer-term interaction within a short session.

Features

  • Care-based interactions (e.g., feeding, petting)
  • Animated responses that reflect the pet’s state
  • A simple trust progression system over time
  • A chat interface to simulate conversational interaction (Wizard-of-Oz during testing)
